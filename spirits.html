Spirits

Gin

Gin is a spirit known for its juniper berry flavor. While other botanicals are added in addition to juniper berries, such as lemon and lavendor, your opinion on the juniper berry flavor more or less determines your love, or lack there of, for gin. Similar to how some unfortunate souls are genetically doomed to find that cilantro tastes like soap, there are those that find gin tastes like pinesol.

For my money, there is no better wedding reception drink than a G&T.

Tequila

Tequilla is a spirit that originated in Mexico distilled from the agave plant. For some reason tequilla seems to be the most confusing spirit for people I know to buy. So let me give a few buying tips. If affordable, only buy tequilla made from 100% agave. There are three types of tequilla all great for different things.
Blanco: Aged less than 2 months. Great for mixed drinks like margaritas.
Reposado: Aged for 2 months to 1 year. Have a more mellow flavor than blanco. Can be drank on its own or in a cocktail.
Anejo: Aged for 1 to 3 years. A good anejo tequilla is excellent for sipping. You can certainly use it for cocktails but it is worth giving it a try on its own with some ice.
Gold is not a classic type of tequilla. Gold is not reccomended for anything including margaritas.

Vodka

Vodka, which originated in Russia, is a spirit distilled from rye, wheat or potatoes. Historically, the idea of vodka has been to have no flavor at all, making it easy to enjoy. A traditional way to enjoy Vodka is chilled with a pickle chaser. A screwdrive, vodka and orange juice, is a standard first drink for many new to drinking. Now there are plenty of flavored options as well from fruit flavors to some that taste like "birthday cake". Enjoy the latter at your own risk.

Whiskey

Whiskey, or whisky, is a spirit distilled from malted grain, generally barley or rye. If you have a friend that likes whiskey then you likely have also heard the "correct" way to enjoy it. Whether it is a splash of water, neat or with a single large ice cube to stop it from being watered down, among a few others, there are multitudes of ways to enjoy different varieties of whiskey. Try a bunch and find out what you prefer. You will likely have different preferences based on the type of whiskey. Lastly, justify because it is more expensive does not make it better. There are $20 whiskies that are superior to $40 ones. Prices have gone a little high over the past few years due to an increasing demand.
