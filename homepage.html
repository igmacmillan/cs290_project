Mission

The purpose of this site is to share my enjoyment of and limited, some would say extremley so, knowledge of spirits. This site is by no means meant to convey the "correct" or "proper" way to enjoy a spirit. As a famous fake internet judge says: "People like what they like".

To further this sites purpose feedback is included right on the homepage. If you have suggestions related to design or content, or if you just want to pass along well wishes please feel free to provide those here. I only ask that you keep the suggestions civil and that they would be constructive suggestions you would want to hear in my place.

Poems For Your Spirits

Sloe Gin

The clear weather of juniper darkened into winter. She fed gin to sloes and sealed the glass container.

When I unscrewed it I smelled the disturbed tart stillness of a bush rising through the pantry.

When I poured it it had a cutting edge and flamed like Betelgeuse.

I drink to you in smoke-mirled, blue- black sloes, bitter and dependable.

-Seamus Heaney

A Drinking Song

Wine comes in at the mouth
And love comes in at the eye;
That’s all we shall know for truth
Before we grow old and die.
I lift the glass to my mouth,
I look at you, and I sigh.

-William Butler Yeats

Be Drunk

You have to be always drunk. That’s all there is to it—it’s the only way. So as not to feel the horrible burden of time that breaks your back and bends you to the earth, you have to be continually drunk.

But on what? Wine, poetry or virtue, as you wish. But be drunk.

And if sometimes, on the steps of a palace or the green grass of a ditch, in the mournful solitude of your room, you wake again, drunkenness already diminishing or gone, ask the wind, the wave, the star, the bird, the clock, everything that is flying, everything that is groaning, everything that is rolling, everything that is singing, everything that is speaking. . .ask what time it is and wind, wave, star, bird, clock will answer you: “It is time to be drunk! So as not to be the martyred slaves of time, be drunk, be continually drunk! On wine, on poetry or on virtue as you wish.”

-Charles Baudelaire
